Peanut soup

Description

This is one of the most popular Bolivian traditional dishes, and was shot back into the national culinary consciousness on the famous TV show Master Chef.

It is prepared with white peanuts, various vegetables, macaroni, pieces of beef or chicken, and served with French fries which float on top as a garnish.

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbsp salt
  • 4 beef ribs or bone-in cut of beef
  • 1 small red onion
  • 2 small carrots
  • 1/2 green pepper, medium dice
  • 1/4 red bell pepper, medium dice
  • 10 green beans, sliced diagonally across for long, thin ovals
  • 1/2 pound skinless raw peanuts
  • 4 Yukon potatoes
  • 1/2 cup white rice
  • 1 big clove garlic
  • 1/2 tsp powdered cumin
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • Handful fresh cilantro
  • Handful fresh parsley
  • Small bunch fresh celery leaves
  • 1/4 cup peas
  • Crusty bread
  • Llajua (a fresh hot sauce condiment)
  • 1 tomato
  • 1 jalapeño
  • Small handful cilantro
  • 1 tsp dried oregano

Steps

  1. Fill a soup pot 2/3 full of water, add 1 Tbsp salt and beef. Bring to boil and simmer for 1-2 hours (longer for tougher cuts of meat). Keep a lid on to prevent broth from evaporating too much. Skim any fat and foam that rise to the top of the soup.
  2. While the meat broth is brewing, cut your veggies. Cut carrots lengthwise into 1/4″ thick planks, then crosswise into 1/4″ strips. Dice green and red pepper and onion. Cut green beans on the diagonal. Put the veggies in the soup pot.
  3. Make a raw peanut puree by blending the peanuts in a blender with about a cup of water until you have a creamy consistency. Add the peanut puree to the soup after the meat has cooked for at least an hour.
  4. Continue cooking for an hour, stirring occasionally. Mash garlic, 1/4 tsp pepper, and 1/2 tsp cumin in a mortar and pestle. Add to the soup.
  5. Prep for soup garnishes. Make a bowl of fresh feathery herbs by gathering parsley and cilantro and cutting them toward your thumb with a paring knife. Fry potato strips.
  6. Make fried potato strips by slicing potatoes into thin strips, frying until golden brown, and salting them.
  7. Make homemade hotsauce (llajua) by pulsing fresh jalapeños, tomatoes, and cilantro in a blender. Avoid blending on full blast to prevent foaming.
  8. After peanuts have simmered with the soup for about an hour, add rice. After rice has cooked for about ten minutes, tie a bouquet of celery leaves and parsley leaves with cooking twine and steep in the soup.
  9. Take meat out of the pot, pull meat off the bone, discard bone, and put meat back in the soup.
  10. When the rice is cooked, add peas and sprinkle dried oregano over the top. Taste the soup and add salt as needed.
  11. Serve soup in shallow bowls, sprinkle fried potatoes in the center of each bowl, and add fresh herbs on top. Serve with chunks of baguette and llajua on the table.
