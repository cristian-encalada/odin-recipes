Silpancho

Description

Typical of the Cochabamba valley, this dish consists of a piece of beef beaten until it is very thin. It is accompanied by white rice, fried egg, boiled and browned potatoes, plus a raw sauce based on tomato, onion, and locoto. One of the features you’ll notice if you order this is the huge size of the portions.

This enormous meal has become a feature of Cochabamba’s nightlife. Because hungry revelers needed an economical late-night snack served in a way they could take away with them, the Trancapecho was born.

You may notice that this curious concoction resembles a Silpancho. However, served in crusty rustic bread, it’s known as the “poor son of Silpancho”. Still, it will fill you up just as well!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup white rice
  • 3 Yukon gold potatoes
  • 1 small green pepper, medium diced
  • 1/2 small red onion, medium diced
  • 1 tomato, medium diced
  • 2 tsp vinegar
  • 2 tsp vegetable oil
  • 1 lb ground beef (with the least fat content possible, ideally 90% or more lean, buffalo meat is also delicious)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 3/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • Handful of Quillquiña leaves (or cilantro)
  • Canola oil

Steps

  1. First, cook 1 cup of rice with 2 cups of water on the stove.
  2. Boil 3 whole potatoes for about 10 minutes. Once cool enough to touch, slice them.
  3. Massage salt and pepper into the ground beef. Divide the ground beef into balls the size of limes.
  4. Sprinkle ground pepper onto a pile of breadcrumbs, then roll each beef ball in the breadcrumbs for a light coating.
  5. Coat each side of the meat with breadcrumbs, and roll flat with a rolling pin on top of the breadcrumbs, flipping when it starts to stick.
  6. The final thickness should be like a crepe. Roll each meatball out into a sheet and stack the rolled meat sheets on a plate.
  7. Pan-sear each meat sheet on medium-high, flipping when you see raw pink start to turn brown.
  8. Stack each finished meat sheet on a plate, then fry eggs individually and brown the potato rounds.
  9. Top the dish with a salad of tomato, red onion, and green pepper. Dress with a mixture of equal parts vinegar and oil, plus generous salt.
  10. Put the plate together by layering potatoes at the bottom, then rice, the beef, egg, and finally the salad.
  11. Prepare a spicy llajwa (sauce) to go with it!
